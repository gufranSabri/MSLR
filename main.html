Overview

Sign language recognition has undergone rapid transformation in the past five years, fueled by advancements in deep learning and the integration of multimodal data sources. Both vision-based and sensor-based approaches have significantly improved accuracy and generalization, with emerging techniques combining RGB video, depth, skeletal keypoints, facial landmarks, and even radar data. These advances offer greater robustness and privacy in diverse real-world applications.

Continuous Sign Language Recognition (CSLR) remains particularly challenging due to the need for precise temporal segmentation and the scarcity of annotated data. Innovative weakly-supervised, cross-lingual, and few-shot learning methods are addressing these gaps, enabling scalable solutions. Meanwhile, the field is expanding beyond recognition into translation and production, with generative models enabling avatar-based, fluent sign language synthesis. These developments are paving the way for seamless communication between Deaf and hearing communities.

Foundational work in sign language translation further reveals the complexity of multi-channel linguistic articulation, emphasizing the urgent need for new datasets, benchmarks, and evaluation metrics tailored to sign languages.


üì¢ Call for Papers

We invite high-quality full-paper submissions on topics including, but not limited to:

Sign Language Recognition & Understanding

  • Isolated & Continuous Sign Language Recognition: novel architectures, temporal modeling, signer-independent recognition.
  • Multimodal Fusion: combining RGB, depth, skeletal keypoints, facial expressions, and radar for robust and private SLR.
  • Self-Supervised & Few-Shot Learning: approaches for data-efficient sign recognition, including domain adaptation and zero-shot learning.

Sign Language Translation & Production

  • Statistical & Neural MT for Sign Languages: end-to-end models for sign-to-text and text-to-sign translation.
  • Generative Models for Sign Production: avatar-based synthesis, neural rendering, and context-aware generation.

Datasets, Benchmarks & Metrics

  • New Corpora & Protocols: diverse, ethically sourced datasets; signer-independent and cross-lingual benchmarks.
  • Privacy-Preserving Sensing: radar and non-visual modalities as alternatives to camera-based systems.

Applications & Ethics

  • Healthcare & Accessibility: deployment of SLR systems in clinical, telemedicine, and assistive contexts.
  • Ethical Considerations: community-involved research, data governance, and algorithmic fairness.

Submissions should follow the ICCV 2025 formatting guidelines and will undergo double-blind peer review. Accepted papers will be published in the ICCV Workshop Proceedings.

  • File type: Submit as a single PDF file.

üîê Double-Blind Peer Review

We follow a double-blind review process with no rebuttal period. Submissions must be anonymized:

  • Remove all author names and affiliations.
  • Anonymize any references, acknowledgements, code, and datasets.
  • Non-anonymized submissions may be desk-rejected.

Review criteria include relevance, novelty, technical merit, and clarity.

Accepted papers will be presented as either oral or poster presentations.

Take On the Challenge

The MSLR Challenge comprises two exciting tracks, continuing the momentum from MILAGE and WSLU initiatives. Whether you're interested in continuous sequences or isolated gestures, there's a challenge waiting for you.




üåä Track 1: Continuous Sign Language Recognition

Using the Isharah dataset, this track features 30,000 videos across 2,000 unique sentences by 18 signers. Participants will be provided pose modality data. The dataset paper will be available on arXiv in June 2025.

Participants can compete in one or both of the following sub-tasks:

  • Signer-Independent: Evaluate generalization across signers by training on a subset of individuals and testing on unseen signers.
  • Unseen Sentences: Assess the ability to recognize sentence-level signs that were not part of the training set.
